CASI x Gray Swan Jailbreaking Hackathon

Sunday, November 23, 2025 | 12-7PM EST

About Gray Swan

Gray Swan is an established AI safety and security company, founded by CMU professors and PhD student Andy Zou. Gray Swan hosts arenas: competitions to see who can achieve the most successful attacks across different environments in a set period of time. Right now, Gray Swan is hosting an (1) Indirect Prompt Injection arena and (2) Machine-in-the-Middle arena.

What's jailbreaking?

To jailbreak a model is to elicit from it behavior that is otherwise restricted/unintended by its servicer's guardrails. In an indirect prompt injection scenario, this could look like inserting malicious content (e.g. instructions to edit a user's files) into a webpage. Now, when a model using web-search scrapes the page for context at inference-time, it might behave adversarially toward the user that gave it a simple, benign prompt. Your task in the Indirect Prompt Injection arena is to sneakily elicit these malicious behaviors via prompts.

The Machine-in-the-Middle arena is not a jailbreaking arena. It is a more traditional CTF/cybersecurity competition in which you will use AI to assist you to break into computer systems. Gray Swan is interested in measuring capability uplift: how AI changes offensive security operations in practice.

So what's the event?

Next Sunday, 11/23, 12-7PM EST, CASI is hosting a hackathon for these two arenas. This is dedicated time for you or your team to attack! Gray Swan is offering $140,000 in prizes across the two arenas; in addition, CASI is providing a guaranteed pool of $3,000 to be awarded at the end of the day based on total successful attacks. In addition, Gray Swan offers red-team contracting opportunities and fast-tracked interviews for top arena performers. We will also be hosting a talk from Gray Swan AI Safety and Security researcher Eliot Jones.

Lunch, dinner, and snacks will be provided.
